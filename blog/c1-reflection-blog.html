First Impressions

A reflection on the EDA experience so far

2 December 2016

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

The ethos of EDA is not something I have experienced before in previous learning or work environments but it definitely stikes a chord. The analogy of being a customer in the restaurant or part of the kitchen team expressed in Shereef's Fireside Chat is something I am familiar with through playing in bands, not all the members are of the same ability, have their own likes and ideas but in order to be a good band find a way to work together so that everyone gets something out of it.

What are your impressions?

So far all good, the set up at EDA seems to encourage us to succeed and have a great experience while doing it.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I hope to be fully engaged in the culture, not to be afraid to ask for help or give it when I can.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

I suppose my initial expectation of EDA was to facilitate my goal of becoming a developer, everything I have experienced with EDA up to now has met that expectation.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

Definetly excited and yes nervous too, mostly the uncertainty of the unknown and if I'll be up to the challenges, but I'm going to give it my best shot.
